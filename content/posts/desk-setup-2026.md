After some tinkering, my current desk setup has settled into a comfortable spot. At the core is a dual-monitor arrangement sitting on a wooden desk. A bamboo organizer keeps the surface tidy, doubling as a small platform for a compact device — wired up with the usual mix of GPIO-coloured cables that signal a Raspberry Pi or similar single-board computer lurking underneath.

A power strip keeps everything fed without hunting for wall sockets, and an ergonomic mesh chair finishes the picture for the long coding sessions.

Nothing exotic, but it works: clean sightlines to both screens, easy reach to the SBC for whatever project is running on it, and enough cable management to feel in control rather than fighting entropy.

The bamboo stand is worth calling out — it raises the secondary board just enough to reduce desk clutter while keeping connectors accessible. A small detail that makes a noticeable difference over a full day of work.
